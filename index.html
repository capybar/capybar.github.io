Okay. I'm trying to seem professional with a nice professional shiny page. My name is Rachel and I'm a web designer currently located in the Capital Region. Some people call me The Britt-Busler...because I'm hella fierce and do everything with immense intensity. How do I do that you ask? First by never leaving a digial screen-be it computer or tablet, and second by always drinking coffee #thankyoustarbucks (I can hashtag that because I work there). One day I hope to be able to never be stuck in one place and afford real pets as opposed to having fake digital cats (pls don't be offended my Neko Katsumis!)

I first entered the world of code through in middle school when I was introduced to the wonder world of Blog Spot (now Blogger). Let's be honest, it was shit. Google has a great search engine-but its social media platforms aren't really great. I remebered I hated that way the blog looked. But the site didn't allow for much customization. At the time, I wasn't rich enough to afford my own custom domain name and hosting site so I looked towards Wordpress. However Wordpress only allowed modification if you payed a premium page.This was the first book I read in its entirety. I discovered Tumblr in my freshmen year of college. I started by modifying tumblr themes and reading up tutorials by Monique Tendencia. Soon after I began to make my own themes.

Colophon

This is an incomplete indespensible list of resources I use to create and expand my knowledge of web design and Tumblr them deveolopemnt

Infinite Scroll by Paul Irish : Most of my themes don’t include this but the earlier ones did. I’m personally not a fan of infinite scroll

Unsplash: Almost all of the photos are taken from there. I also get a lot of inspiration from them

Smithsonian Women in Science I think it’s important to consider the stock photographs we designers decide to use. I’ve noticed with many themes that the icons tend to be of men designing things. I honestly just don’t see enough women or scientists. Being that I consider myself both, I like to incorporate these amazing role models in my themes.

Mathjax: This is the magical code that lets you write math in your blog posts. Unfortunately it requires a bit of LaTeX and JQuery knowledge. But it’s the best I’ve got. I know writing math is more of a useless feature in my themes and no one really uses it, but I love the option.

Packery: Many theme developers use Masonry (and I’ve also dabbled in it) but I I prefer the easy integration and the ability to initiate code from the HTML of Packery. It’s easy to execute but can be difficult to understand at times.

CoolStoryTip This is a tooltip by Amit Jakhu. It’s only been used in a few of my themes.

Google Fonts An indefensible resource probably the most important for theme developement  The fonts used most are Bitter, Abriel Fatface, Mondo, Open Sans, Raleway, Playfair Display

Beautiful Web Type I am not a typography expert, so I stare at this site often to help me decide how to pair fonts.

Font Awesome I use this for social media icons

Ingrid Ulliel Icons Discovered this through Fukuo. It’s useful stock icons.

Brackets I use this for coding
